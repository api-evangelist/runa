<p>Earlier this month, we turned our London HQ into something more than just an office, it became a space for real product talk. No buzzwords. No fluff. Just sharp insights and honest conversation at our first-ever Runa Product Event.</p>
<p><span id="more-27112"></span></p>
<p>After July’s tech-forward gathering, this time we invited the product community in for a fresh kind of exchange. The theme, <strong>Beyond the Hype</strong>: What really matters in B2B Fintech product development.</p>
<p>It wasn’t about chasing trends. It was about building what lasts: designing responsibly, leading with empathy, and delivering value that sticks. And the response? Electric. A packed room, thoughtful questions, and conversations that kept going long after the last talk.</p>
<p>Here’s what stood out.</p>
<h3>The Talks</h3>
<p><strong>Foodtech → Fintech: What’s Different vs What Stays the Same</strong><strong><br />
</strong> <strong>Meghna Vadapalli, Senior Product Manager @ Runa</strong></p>
<p>Meghna opened the night by charting her path from food delivery to digital payouts. Turns out, navigating a three-sided ecosystem and building API-first products crosses industry lines, and brings crucial learnings with it.</p>
<p>Her take? Domain changes can stretch you, but the fundamentals of product thinking stay strong.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Key takeaway:</span> Switching industries isn’t starting over. It’s building on what works, and being brave enough to adapt what doesn’t.</p>
<p><strong>Designing for B2B vs B2C: Providing Value Without Dark Patterns<br />
Antoniya Kuneva, Senior Product Designer @ Runa</strong></p>
<p>Next, Antoniya zoomed in on the ethical edges of design, especially the divide between B2C tricks and B2B trust.</p>
<p>In a world where clarity builds loyalty, she unpacked why dark patterns don’t belong in our toolkit, and how Runa prioritises partnership over quick wins.</p>
<p style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Key takeaway:</span> B2B users aren’t just end-points. They’re decision-makers. Partners. Respect goes further than any pop-up ever could.</p>
<p style="font-weight: normal;">
<p style="font-weight: normal;"><strong>Don’t Leave Empathy Behind: Bridging Strategy and Real Use Cases<br />
Lina Kandinova, Lead Product Manager @ Runa</strong></p>
<p>Lina closed the evening with a grounded reminder: Empathy isn’t just a nice-to-have. It’s a product imperative.</p>
<p>Through lived examples from Runa, she showed how understanding real constraints leads to sharper strategy, faster pivots, and fewer product misfires.</p>
<p style="font-weight: normal;"><strong>Key takeaway: </strong>In high-stakes ecosystems, empathy is your edge.</p>
<h3>Q&amp;A, Pizza and the Good Stuff</h3>
<p>Our audience didn’t hold back, bringing thoughtful, sharp questions that sparked lively back-and-forths on trade-offs, roadmap bets, and what it <em>actually</em> takes to ship.</p>
<p>And yes, we ended with the classics: pizza, drinks, and the kind of conversations that stretch long past the agenda.</p>
<p>Big thanks to everyone who stayed late to swap stories and make this first event what it was.</p>
<h3>What&#8217;s Next?</h3>
<p>We’re just getting started. Runa was built on open conversations, about what’s working, what’s shifting, and what needs a rethink in digital payouts and B2B product development.</p>
